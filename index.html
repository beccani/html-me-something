A Vision Board for My Future House

I've gotten to a point that I no longer want to pay rent to a landlord, but also don't have the means to take out a mortgage for a new home. It is a pipe dream of mine to build my own home without taking out a loan to do so. This is a rough outline of what that process would look like.

The Process

  1. Acquire Land
  2. Prepare the Land
  3. Build Initial Dwelling
  4. Skeleton of House
  5. The Interior

1. Acquire Land

Some attributes I would like the land to have:

  • A good balance between close to town and away from the activity
  • Have a creek running through it
  • Easy road access, but tucked far enough away from any main roads
  • A view would be nice...

2. Prepare the Land

To prepare for the construction, I would need to clear any land that I intended to build on, but would want to plan the construction around the vegetation that was already present. I don't want to cut down any healthy trees or hearty vegetation. I would also want to explore and see if I found any interesting features on the land that I would need to plan around such as rock bluffs, old cellars, natural springs, etc.
I would also need to ensure that the proper utilities were installed and prepared for the land.

3. Build Initial Dwelling

There a few different ways I could accomplish this:
♦build a garage type building that would have the bare minimum utilities (ideally it would have a loft as the living space. This would keep the garage space free for tools and materials for the construction.)
♦buy a RV/camper and park it on the land
♦begin the house as a small unit to extend on
I get most excited about the garage type building with a loft, but can't seem to find an example of what I have in mind.

4. Skeleton of House

Ok, so from here on out, these are all just stabs in the dark as far as what the process would look like since I don't have experience building houses.
The house must have:

  • A mud room
  • A study room/office space
  • 2 bedrooms
  • A porch area, for front-porch sittin'
